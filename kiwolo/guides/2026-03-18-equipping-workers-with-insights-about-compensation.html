Equipping workers with insights about compensation

March 18, 2026 · View Source ↗

What It Is

OpenAI's research initiative, "Equipping Workers with Insights About Compensation," reveals a remarkable behavioral trend: Americans are sending nearly 3 million messages per day to ChatGPT specifically to ask questions about compensation, salaries, wages, and earnings. This finding, published on the OpenAI Blog, highlights how conversational AI is rapidly becoming one of the most trusted and accessible tools for closing the longstanding wage information gap — the systemic disparity in compensation knowledge between employers and employees.

At its core, this is not a new product launch but rather a data-driven research finding that documents how workers are already using ChatGPT as a personal compensation analyst. From asking "What is the average salary for a software engineer in Austin, Texas?" to more nuanced queries like "How should I negotiate a raise after two years without a promotion?", workers across industries are turning to AI to level the informational playing field that has historically favored employers.

The phenomenon intersects several domains: labor economics, AI ethics, conversational AI capabilities, and workforce empowerment. It positions ChatGPT not just as a productivity tool or coding assistant, but as a democratizing force in the labor market — one that gives individual workers access to the kind of data-informed insights previously reserved for HR departments, compensation consultants, and corporate leadership teams.

Key Components of This Research

  • Usage Volume: ~3 million daily compensation-related messages sent to ChatGPT by American users
  • Topic Coverage: Salary benchmarking, raise negotiation, benefits comparison, career transition pay, freelance rate setting
  • Access Mechanism: Primarily through ChatGPT (chat.openai.com) and the ChatGPT mobile apps
  • Underlying Models: GPT-4o and GPT-4 Turbo, trained on vast datasets including labor market information

Why It Matters

The significance of this research extends far beyond a simple usage statistic. For decades, information asymmetry in compensation has been one of the most persistent structural inequalities in labor markets. Employers have always had access to salary surveys, compensation benchmarking tools (like Radford, Mercer, or Culpepper), and HR analytics platforms. Individual workers, by contrast, have historically relied on informal networks, occasional Glassdoor reviews, or sheer luck to understand their market value.

Closing the Wage Information Gap

The wage information gap disproportionately affects:

  • Women and minorities, who are statistically less likely to negotiate salaries aggressively due to less access to benchmarking data
  • Workers in smaller cities and rural areas, where local salary data is sparse
  • Career changers, who lack industry-specific networks to gauge fair compensation
  • Gig and freelance workers, who must price their services without employer-provided frameworks

ChatGPT's ability to synthesize compensation data, explain negotiation strategies, and provide context-aware advice at scale represents a structural shift in how labor market information flows. The fact that 3 million messages per day are being sent on this topic alone suggests that workers are actively seeking this empowerment — and finding it useful enough to return to repeatedly.

Novelty in the AI Landscape

What makes this particularly significant from an AI perspective is the conversational, personalized nature of the interaction. Unlike a static salary database, ChatGPT can:

  • Tailor advice based on years of experience, location, company size, and industry
  • Walk users through negotiation scripts in real time
  • Explain the "why" behind compensation ranges
  • Help users draft negotiation emails or prepare for difficult conversations

Step-by-Step Usage Guide

Step 1: Establish Your Context Profile

Before asking compensation questions, provide ChatGPT with your professional context. The more specific you are, the more tailored the response.

Prompt example:

"I am a mid-level data analyst with 4 years of experience, 
specializing in SQL, Python, and Tableau. I work in the 
healthcare industry in Chicago, Illinois, at a company with 
approximately 500 employees. What is a competitive salary 
range for my role and location?"

Step 2: Request Benchmarking Data with Source Context

Ask ChatGPT to break down compensation by percentile and explain what drives variance in pay ranges.

Prompt example:

"For a Senior Product Manager in San Francisco with 6+ years 
of experience at a Series B startup, can you break down the 
typical compensation structure including:
1. Base salary range (25th, 50th, 75th percentile)
2. Equity expectations (stock options or RSUs)
3. Bonus structure
4. Total compensation range

Also explain what factors push someone to the higher end 
of these ranges."

Step 3: Generate a Negotiation Strategy

Use ChatGPT to build a personalized negotiation script based on your situation and the offer on the table.

Prompt example:

"I received a job offer for a Software Engineer II role at 
a fintech company in New York. The offer is:
- Base: $115,000
- Bonus: 10%
- RSUs: $40,000 over 4 years

Based on market data, I believe the fair range is 
$125,000-$135,000 base. Write me a professional 
negotiation email that is confident but collaborative, 
referencing market data without being confrontational."

Step 4: Run Scenario Comparisons

Use ChatGPT to compare job offers, calculate the real value of benefits packages, or model career trajectory pay projections.

Prompt example:

"Compare these two job offers for a Marketing Manager role 
and tell me which has better total compensation and long-term 
financial value:

Offer A: $95,000 base, 15% bonus, full health coverage, 
4 weeks PTO, no equity, 401k with 3% match

Offer B: $88,000 base, 20% bonus, health coverage with 
$200/month premium, 3 weeks PTO, $20,000 RSUs over 3 years, 
401k with 5% match

I am 30 years old and plan to stay at least 3 years."

Step 5: Prepare for the Raise Conversation

Prompt example:

"I want to ask for a 15% raise at my annual review next month. 
I am a UX Designer with 3 years at my current company, 
and I have led 2 major product redesigns that increased 
conversion by 23%. Help me:
1. Build a business case for my raise
2. Anticipate my manager's objections
3. Know my walk-away number
4. Practice the conversation with a role-play script"

An independent data scientist wants to price a 3-month analytics consulting contract appropriately.

Prompt:

machine learning for e-commerce companies? 
I am considering both project-based and retainer pricing models."

Use Case 3: HR Professional Benchmarking Internal Pay Bands

An HR manager at a 200-person SaaS company uses ChatGPT to quickly draft pay band structures before purchasing an expensive compensation study.

Prompt:
"Help me create a preliminary pay band structure for a 
B2B SaaS company with 200 employees headquartered in 
Denver. I need bands for:
- Software Engineering (L1-L5)
- Product Management (Associate, Mid, Senior, Principal)
- Customer Success (Associate, Mid, Senior, Manager)

Include base salary ranges and typical equity grants per level."

Use Case 4: Career Changer Evaluating Industry Switch ROI

A teacher considering a move into instructional design wants to understand the financial implications of the career change.

Prompt:
"I am a high school teacher earning $58,000 per year in 
Ohio with 8 years of experience. If I transition to 
corporate instructional design or L&D, what salary can 
I realistically expect in year 1, year 3, and year 5? 
What certifications or skills would accelerate my earnings?"

Use Case 5: Expat Evaluating International Compensation Packages

A software engineer evaluating a relocation package from the US to Germany wants to understand the true purchasing-power-adjusted value of the offer.

Prompt:
"I earn $140,000 in Seattle as a software engineer. 
I have a job offer in Berlin, Germany for €90,000 per year. 
Accounting for tax differences, cost of living, 
universal healthcare benefits, and purchasing power parity, 
is this offer financially equivalent, better, or worse 
than my current US compensation?"

Use Case 6: Manager Building an Equity Compensation Counter-Offer

A Director of Engineering trying to retain a key employee uses ChatGPT to model equity refresh scenarios.

Prompt:
"One of my senior engineers has a competing offer with 
$50,000 more in RSUs over 4 years. Our company is Series C. 
Help me model a counter-offer with an equity refresh 
that is financially competitive but accounts for our 
current 409A valuation of $8 per share and typical 
dilution at our stage."

Code Examples

Example 1: Python Script — Automated Compensation Research Tool Using OpenAI API

import openai
import json

# Initialize the OpenAI client
client = openai.OpenAI(api_key="your-api-key-here")

def get_compensation_insights(
    job_title: str,
    location: str,
    years_experience: int,
    industry: str,
    company_size: str
) -> dict:
    """
    Query ChatGPT for detailed compensation insights
    based on user-provided professional profile.
    """

    system_prompt = """You are an expert compensation analyst 
    with deep knowledge of US labor market data, salary 
    surveys (Radford, Mercer, Glassdoor, LinkedIn Salary, 
    Bureau of Labor Statistics). Provide accurate, 
    data-informed compensation insights. Always return 
    structured JSON."""

    user_prompt = f"""
    Provide a compensation analysis for the following profile:
    - Job Title: {job_title}
    - Location: {location}
    - Years of Experience: {years_experience}
    - Industry: {industry}
    - Company Size: {company_size}

    Return a JSON object with:
    {{
        "salary_range": {{
            "p25": <25th percentile>,
            "p50": ,
            "p75": <75th percentile>,
            "p90": <90th percentile>
        }},
        "typical_bonus_percent": ,
        "equity_expectation": "",
        "key_factors_for_higher_pay": ["factor1", "factor2"],
        "negotiation_tips": ["tip1", "tip2", "tip3"],
        "market_trend": ""
    }}
    """

    response = client.chat.completions.create(
        model="gpt-4o",
        messages=[
            {"role": "system", "content": system_prompt},
            {"role": "user", "content": user_prompt}
        ],
        temperature=0.3,  # Lower temperature for factual responses
        response_format={"type": "json_object"}
    )

    result = json.loads(response.choices[0].message.content)
    return result


def format_compensation_report(data: dict, profile: dict) -> str:
    """Format the API response into a readable report."""
    report = f"""
    ====================================================
    COMPENSATION ANALYSIS REPORT
    ====================================================
    Role: {profile['job_title']} | {profile['location']}
    Experience: {profile['years_experience']} years
    Industry: {profile['industry']}
    Company Size: {profile['company_size']}
    ----------------------------------------------------
    SALARY RANGES (Annual Base):
      25th Percentile: ${data['salary_range']['p25']:,}
      Median (50th):   ${data['salary_range']['p50']:,}
      75th Percentile: ${data['salary_range']['p75']:,}
      90th Percentile: ${data['salary_range']['p90']:,}

    Typical Bonus: {data['typical_bonus_percent']}% of base
    Equity: {data['equity_expectation']}

    MARKET TREND:
      {data['market_trend']}

    TOP FACTORS FOR HIGHER PAY:
    {chr(10).join(f"  • {f}" for f in data['key_factors_for_higher_pay'])}

    NEGOTIATION TIPS:
    {chr(10).join(f"  {i+1}. {t}" for i, t in enumerate(data['negotiation_tips']))}
    ====================================================
    """
    return report
